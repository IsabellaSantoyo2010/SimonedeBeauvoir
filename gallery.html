Simone de Beauvoir was born into a Parisian, bourgeois family with Catholic and conservative values that experienced financial ruin during her youth. Her father, Georges Bertrand de Beauvoir, was a lawyer and intellectual who valued culture but held patriarchal views, while her mother, Françoise Brasseur, was from a wealthy family and was deeply religious. These contrasting ideologies and the family's economic downturn significantly influenced Beauvoir's feminist perspective and critique of societal norms. She maintained a lifelong, unconventional partnership with philosopher Jean-Paul Sartre, deliberately choosing not to marry or have children of her own.
She and her lifelong intellectual partner Jean-Paul Sartre maintained an open, non-marital "soul partnership". Her legacy and estate were eventually passed to her adopted daughter, Sylvie Le Bon-de Beauvoir, a philosophy professor whom Beauvoir legally adopted in 1980. Sylvie Le Bon-de Beauvoir became the sole executor of Beauvoir's will and has since published several volumes of her letters and works.
Lucía Isabella Santoyo 2025
